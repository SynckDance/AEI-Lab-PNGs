Figure 4 — AEI Activation Framework (AAF)
01 Reconstructive
Uses stored movement data to resurrect extinct or endangered dances. QTC, SAM, and generative models infer missing transitions while respecting stylistic integrity.
Example
Reconstruct lost Efik initiation dances by aligning surviving sequences with related forms
02 Diagnostic
Treats movement as a window into physiological and psychological states. Correlates movement qualities with patterns of fatigue, injury, or emotional state.
Example
Detect early signs of neurological conditions through balance, rhythm, and symmetry changes
03 Cybernetic
Uses movement as biometric signature. Kinetic profiles can authenticate identity, serve as authorship marks, or function as embodied encryption.
Example
A choreographer's movement phrase operates as kinesthetic password or licensing signature
04 Speculative
Explores how dances might evolve in future ecologies where humans, machines, and environments co-create. Ethics and imagination must be co-designed.
Example
Simulate how a step adapts across ages, climates, or post-anthropocentric conditions
all dimensions governed by PPC ethics + custodian review
The AEI Activation Framework organizes how archived movement can be activated beyond storage. Each dimension carries distinct ethical stakes—reconstructive work requires community authority; diagnostic use demands consent and care; cybernetic applications must resist surveillance; speculative modeling must acknowledge uncertainty.
